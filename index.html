The F1 Files

Formula One is an internationally renowned racing series of open-wheel, single-seater formula racing cars. This sport has especially risen in popularity in recent years with the release of Netflix's Drive to Survive, and the F1 movie. Yet, the sport is not without its challenges, from sexism and racism in Motorsport, extreme exclusivity, and more. This is where the F1 Files come in: this site serves as an accessible informational page on each team, driver, and circuit. F1 is an excellent educational platform for all, from business skills on the team side, engineering skills for the car, learning resilience, mental fortitude, and the importance of teamwork from the drivers, etc. Though this can all seem extremely complex, this site aims to make it as easy to find and understand information as possible.

About F1

Formula 1 is the pinnacle of open‑wheel racing, with twenty drivers in ten teams battling across a global calendar of races. Points are given to each driver, up to 25 for a win and down to 1 for tenth, deciding the Driver’s World Championship and Teams Constructor Championship. Each race can be broken down into the Practice Sessions, Qualifying or a sprint race (a shorter race designed for more flat out, aggressive racing) to set the starting grid, and the main race. F1 masters the art of aerodynamics, tire strategy, lightning‑fast pit stops, and in‑race tactics like DRS overtakes and safety‑car restarts.
